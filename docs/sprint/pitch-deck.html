The Problem

Installers run their business across three to five tools that don't talk.

Quoting, CRM, scheduling, project management and STC compliance live in separate systems. The same job data is re-entered across each one — so leads get lost when it's busy, follow-ups slip, and schedules break under weather delays and product substitutions.

Why It Matters

This isn't admin friction — it's frozen cash and wasted spend.

The disconnect hits a small installer where it hurts most: working capital, and money paid to work around tools that were never built for solar.

Why Now

A problem I proved was fixable twenty years ago — and it's only gotten worse.

At Envirogroup, process work alone cut the same data from 13 entry points to 3, and quote turnaround from 30 minutes to 2. Two decades on it still hasn't been productised. The market just sprawled — more tools that each do most of the job, so installers keep buying the next one.

The Solution

One platform, built only for solar.

CurrentFlow replaces the three-to-five tools installers juggle today. Enter job data once, then carry it from lead → quote → install → invoice with no re-entry.

Fast on-site quoting
Professional quotes in minutes, on tablet or mobile.
STC built in
Entitlements calculated and tracked on every job — no lost rebate cash.
Subcontractor logins
Subbies get their own job view without access to the whole business.
Connected scheduling
One pipeline from sale to install — no manual handoffs.
Where Today's Tools Fall Short

Purpose-built beats bolted-together.

Pylon
No subcontractor login. Expensive at scale. Not built for the Australian compliance workflow.
OpenSolar
Quoting-focused. Weak on CRM, scheduling and job management.
Generic CRMs
Bloated and not solar-specific — plus five-figure customisation bills (up to $130k).
Spreadsheets / WhatsApp
Flexible but break under volume. No audit trail, no automation.
CurrentFlow priced at or below the combined cost of the tools it replaces — consolidating spend, not adding to it.
Who It's For

Beachhead: growth-stage and subcontractor-led installers.

Persona B — Growth-Stage Operator
10–30 staff, 20–60 installs/month. Already pays for Pylon plus other tools. Losing revenue in the gaps between them. The owner is the integration layer — and that doesn't scale.
Persona D — Subcontractor-Led Operator
Runs jobs through subbies. No way to give them job visibility without exposing the whole business. Structurally under-served by every existing tool.

Owner-operators (Persona A) self-select over time; enterprise (Persona C) is a later expansion target.
